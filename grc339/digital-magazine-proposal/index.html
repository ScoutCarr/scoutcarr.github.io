Introduction

Echoes of the Underground

Before indie music filled playlists and festival stages, it thrived in dim basements, burned CDs, and late-night blog posts. The early 2000s were a turning point-when artists like Death Cab for Cutie, The Strokes, and Modest Mouse reshaped the indie landscape with raw emotion, DIY grit, and a fearless sense of style. In this issue of Echoes of the Underground, we revisit the pioneers who paved the way for today's indie bands, tracing their influence on the genre's evolving sound and spirit. These are the voices that built the foundation-one song, show, and mixtape at a time.
